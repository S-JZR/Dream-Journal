# Art Museum Excursion Gone Wrong

influences: I always had spare tissues in secondary school and sometimes students asked me for some.
lessons: People turn to those who are most prepared in desperate situations.

I was one of many secondary school students from art class on an excursion to tour an art museum in France that was divided between two isolated buildings. Everyone lined up to enter through metal detectors at the reception when seven of us at the back without white gloves realized only those wearing them were allowed to enter. A male student approached me to my left.

> **Man**: Do you have any spare gloves?
> 

> **Me**: No, but I might be able to make some.
> 

I crouched down beside my school bag and pulled out various white and light grey plastic bags to cut the tops off. I distributed the pieces to those nearby who wore them tightly over their hands and we moved past as nobody noticed.

Halfway through the tour, those of us with fake gloves climbed onto the roof and walked across a long wooden plank between the museum buildings with the street below. We jumped across the front window ledges to try finding an open window to get back inside.
