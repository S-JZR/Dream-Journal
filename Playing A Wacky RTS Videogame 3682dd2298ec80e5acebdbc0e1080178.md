# Description

I was playing an RTS videogame where we fought on realistic maps including grand canyons and castles. Everyone was a real person instead of their avatars. Battles were fought over the multiple rounds where players received varying amounts of money to purchase different tiers of units and defences which all had specific use cases, and whoever won became stronger by gaining some advantage. Different units included basic (green; e.g., light soldier, heavy soldier, and grenadier), epic (pink; e.g., rocketeer and engineer), and legendary (gold; e.g., special forces like a tactical sniper). Different defences included giant walls that blocked pathways and units could stand on.

In round one, four teams of units stood together together on a beach in front of a grand canyon and were fighting to the death. I joined the game late and picked blue because it was one of the larger successful teams.

In round two, we were placed in a castle and all of the losing teams were combined together. There were various cobblestone bridges outside connecting different pieces of land. I noticed a large number of soldiers approaching from the distance and purchased lots of heavy soldiers, grenadiers, a sniper to fight back, and blocked the first bridge with a wall. I was hoping the grenadiers would hinder them by causing lots of splash damage but they spread out each squad and breached the castle.
