# Description

I was one of many university students attending a series of videogame workshops on campus after hearing about it via student emails. The female facilitator assigned us into different teams of five but mine only had three people so I confronted her.

> **Me**: Excuse me, can we be moved into other teams because there’s not enough of us.
> 

> **Woman**: Unless you can get double the amount of students for each team then no.
> 

> **Woman**: It’s time for everyone to brainstorm ideas for a videogame.
> 

We struggled to conceive anything when one of our team members felt ignored and suggested one of his personal projects.

> **Man 1**: But what about my idea?
> 

> **Man 1**: You could all help me complete a prototype I’ve been working on for months about breaking pinatas scattered throughout mini levels.
> 

I didn’t think the idea was good but I resorted to it since there were no other options.

> **Me**: Ok, lets do that.
> 

I overheard someone yelling at me and saw an Asian man, who was a team member from a group assessment in a different class in the dream, seated at the opposite side of the room so I walked over.

> **Man 2**: Hey!
> 

> **Man 2**: I was working alone on the assessment today.
> 

> **Man 2**: Why weren’t you there?
> 

> **Me**: Sorry, I forgot that our class was scheduled at the same time today.
> 

> **Me**: I will change the workshop time slots later to fix it.
> 

> **Me**: Can you send me some tasks to do as homework so I can contribute back.
>
