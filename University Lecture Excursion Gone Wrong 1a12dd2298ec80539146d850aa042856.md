# Description

I was on an art excursion with my secondary school class as we walked in a line through an L-shaped hallway into a large rectangular concrete chicken house with exits on each side of the room and a dirt floor completely covered in in loose pieces of hay and seeds. Suddenly, a swarm of yellow chicks emerged from behind and rushed past my feet. To avoid accidentally stepping on them, I shuffled my feet back and forth, creating a trail as I moved toward the doorway ahead. A few students who took notice mocked me for refusing to walk normally and falling behind. There was a paved walkway outside, beside a row of buildings, that gradually ascended with periodic step and was covered in eggs scattered around. I continued shuffling with controlled force to make them roll away without flinging them into hard surfaces. A few eggs were stood on by others with crumpled shells that revealed bloody areas inside.

After arriving at a university auditorium and taking a seat, a few student volunteers hosted a lecture on designing a good narrative plot. However, the presentation was a mediocre PowerPoint lasting for one minute that showcased the premise of a Sci-Fi story with text on images but lacked any explanation of the creative process behind it. Halfway through, Mrs Brown, my art teacher, sitting a few seats to my left, audibly mentioned how she liked the idea. At the end, only a few people clapped apathetically which was barely audible. They opened another presentation and explained that after lunch they were going to discuss how to design a video game with a theme set in China. The slides were still loading and completely blank so the lead speaker made a cheap gag that perhaps it was made there as the audience responded with reluctant amusement.
