# University Lecture Excursion Gone Wrong

symbols:   • Shuffling Feet: The desire to avoid harming animals and insects.
  • Mrs Brown: The feeling that people are too complacent with university.

I was one of many secondary school students on an art excursion to a university lecture. We entered a chicken house through the back in a line:

- left:
    - exit
    - stables
- front:
    - exit
- right:
    - exit
- back:
    - L-shaped hallway with an exit
- floor:
    - scattered hay and seeds.

Suddenly, a swarm of yellow chicks emerged from the back and rushed past my feet. To avoid accidentally stepping on them, I shuffled my feet back and forth, creating a trail as I moved toward the doorway ahead. A few students took notice and mocked me.

> **Sarajah**: Look at him walking all weird.
> 

> **Woman**: Why are you walking like that?
> 

> **Man 1**: Just walk normal like us.
> 

There was a paved walkway outside, beside a row of buildings, that gradually ascended with periodic step and was covered in eggs scattered around. I continued shuffling with controlled force to make them roll away without flinging them into hard surfaces. A few eggs were stood on by others with crumpled shells that revealed bloody areas inside.

After arriving at a university auditorium I took a seat as a few student volunteers hosted a lecture on designing a good narrative plot. Mrs Brown, my art teacher, was sitting a few seats to my left. The presentation was a mediocre PowerPoint lasting for one minute that showcased the premise of a Sci-Fi story with text on background concept art but lacked any explanation of the creative process or advice. I don’t remember the presentation.

> **Mrs Brown**: (Whispering) Oh, that’s an interesting idea, I like it.
> 

At the end, only a few people clapped apathetically which was barely audible.

> **Man 2**: After lunch you’re all welcome to attend our next presentation about designing videogames with a Chinese theme.
> 

They opened another presentation but the slides were unexpectedly taking longer to load.

> **Man 2**: Perhaps it was made there.
> 

> **Crowd**: Ooohhh…
>
