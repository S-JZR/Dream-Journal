I was one of many secondary students attending a magic show at my former SHOP church hosted by a tubby man with a long beard wearing a black shirt. While forwarding in to get a seat, I noticed that ceiling fan had dropped to the ground but it was still connected to a long cylinder base that extended into the ceiling. The blades were still spinning and had the potential to harm someone. One of my male history teachers from secondary school was standing behind it to make sure that nobody would obliviously walk into it while another male teacher went to the switchboard at the back to turn off that specific fan. I thought the magic tricks were lame because you could see how they worked.

For trick one, the magician had a central giant purple crystal on the ground with mini crystals attached to the sides. He rotated the large crystal and detached the side crystals as if they were mysteriously disconnected as everyone applauded in amazement. I noticed black circle connector platforms under each side crystal spot and I didn’t understand why everyone was oblivious to it.

For trick two, the magician got a male volunteer to stand behind an interior wall at the front with a square rough opening at eye level.

> **Man 1**: Can I get a volunteer?
> 

> **Man 2**: I’ll do it.
> 

> **Man 1**: Walk through the wall.
> 

The volunteer seemingly walked through the wall as it rapidly opened up like one-way saloon doors and shut and everyone applauded.

> **Man 2**: How is that possible?
> 

The magician was busy and didn’t send the volunteer back as he pulled back the doors to expose how the trick worked.
