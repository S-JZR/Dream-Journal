# Description

I was one of many secondary school students on an excursion to my local shopping mall where we had to explore different stores and eventually come up with an original product.

At one side of the mall was a man hosting a kiosk with a robot arm dipping corn cobs into a square tub of secret orange glaze. Some students lined up to purchase the food and buttered it themselves on a chopping board.

> **Bianca**: Wow this tastes perfect.
> 

At the other side of the mall was a spacious 2-storey game room filled with balloons neatly stacked in long dark grey rectangular containers. The goal was to pop as many balloons as possible with three darts to earn points, with those at the back worth more. There were two columns of balloons on the ground worth 5 to 10 points and two rows of balloons on the ceiling worth 300 to 1000 points. Everyone lined up to take turns throwing darts but it became evident that reaching the ceiling was impossible.

> **Me**: It's the American dream.
> 

> **Students**: *Laughing*.
> 

Everyone gathered around white fold-out tables with A3 pages and markers on top to brainstorm a new product but we struggled to think of more than two ideas and resorted to generic words like basketball and croquet.

> **Me**: I think and write down ideas all the time but for some reason nothing is coming to mind.
> 

> **Bianca**: Same.
> 

> **Bianca**: I had many ideas that I thought I would remember but forgot them.
> 

> **Bianca**: I didn’t write them down though.
> 

> **Me**: You really have to write ideas down or you will inevitably forget them and it’s a horrible feeling.
>
