I was one of many people, including strangers and students from secondary school, invited to tour the HQ of a famous muffin company renowned for being eccentric and pushing the boundaries of innovation. The skyscraper was located within a coastal neighbourhood in the USA that featured smooth concrete 2-storey houses with evenly spaced trees on the sidewalk and a black suspension bridge over the ocean to the left. The skyscraper was close to the bridge and about the same height, and the interior had a high-contrast colour scheme. We stood in front of it on the sidewalk when a male tour guide emerged to greet us. I was filming the entire experience with a hand held camera in my left hand.

> **Man 1**: Alright everyone, we're going to head inside and get started.
> 

We followed them inside to journey up multiple floors. 

On floor one, I broke off with the group and entered a light grey side kitchen with a microwave on a bench, a rectangular table with two chairs on the shorter sides, and a male employee. The man heated a large rectangular brown waffle/pancake with a pat of butter on top in the microwave and gave it to me.

> **Man 2**: Take a seat, I will make you a waffle to eat.
> 

I sat down and strategically sliced it into square pieces so it was easier to eat.

> **Man 2**: That’s a good technique.
> 

On floor two, the space was divided into two halves with glass panels along the perimeter. The first half allowed cats to roam freely, sleep in circular beds on cube decorations, and get petted by visitors. The second half was enclosed by a black metal pen and the cats were locked in glass display racks as they watched us. I got the sense that this was a weird artsy Asian company.

On floor three, I encountered various senior executives in a spacious light grey room. An American man sat on a throne atop three steps at the back. I instinctively knew he was the executive vice president and not the CEO. In front of him, three Asian men and one American man sat along the long side of a long rectangular table covered with food. I sat down to eat lunch with them in a single chair on the opposite side as an observer but I don’t remember the conversation they had with each other. I felt concerned about how much of the tour I would be allowed to record but they ignored me. After leaving the room I took two items of food with me in my right hand to eat along the way.

On floor four, I rejoined the tour. The space was divided into multiple cube-painted sections with different colours that each had a central workbench under a spotlight. Everyone was cleaning objects by breaking and scrubbing an egg over them. The yolk had a strange perfume scent that induced a feeling of ecstasy, making our nasal passages and brain tingle so intensely that we had to restrain ourselves.

> **Woman 1**: Wow it smells so good!
> 

> **Woman 2**: I know right.
> 

I didn’t participate and walked around watching everyone work as I ate.

> **Joshua**: I would rather stop and watch [me] because it’s funny that we’re all working hard and he’s just casually still eating.
> 

I didn’t realized that one item in my hand was a bar of soap and took a bite. I couldn’t get the bad taste out of my mouth and left to look for a bathroom to wash my mouth out.

On floor five, I was walking through a white T-junction hallway when I noticed a distressed Taiwanese female student as she turned towards me.

> **Woman 3**: Excuse me, do you know where the toilets are?
> 

I noticed a sign on the wall to the right of her that pointed to the toilets through the middle of the hallway that she missed.

> **Me**: That sign says the toilets are in that direction.
> 

We travelled together and ascended stairs to try finding the toilets.

On the top floor, we exited onto a balcony with glass panel railings and a part of the building made of red bricks with archways. A man who also wanted to find the toilets started following us. We continued ascending a rectangular concrete stairwell and noticed the bridge through an opening at the top. There was a road on top of the bridge and people were walking across it to a grand city mixing neoclassical and modern architecture atop a cliff to the right. There was also a dark yellow train beneath the road that moved past. After noticing how high we were, I was paralyzed with fear and took small steps down while pressing myself against the inner wall.

> **Woman 3**: There is an entire city up here, you should come up!
> 

> **Me**: I don’t think I can continue and I’m making my way back down.
>
