I assumed the role of Mrs Wardle in my grade two class with three boys and one girl seated at desks closer to the front while the rest were empty. I was surprised that these students were normal despite gen alpha having learning difficulties because of excessive screen time. I got tired of the curriculum and decided to deviate into teaching about computers.

> **Me**: Okay everyone, today we will be learning about computers!
> 

> **Me**: Computers are embedded everywhere in the world and shape almost everything we do.
> 

> **Me**: A desktop is a computer.
> 

> **Me**: A smartphone is a …
> 

> **Children**: Computer!
> 

> **Me**: That’s right.
> 

A girl said some nonsensical irrelevant thing about smartphones which I can’t remember.

> Me: Yes, computers do lots of amazing things and compute information.
> 

I was starting to draw simple diagrams of internal and external hardware on two halves of the whiteboard using a blue marker when Mr Broadway, a staff member from secondary school, unexpectedly entered the room and stood silently at the side to observe me. I instinctively knew that I was a new teacher and he was assessing whether I met quality standards. I realized this was a full circle of growing up into an adult, teaching children, and being evaluated. I was unsettled and looked at him as he scowled at me. I wondered if he was trying to insinuate something and I returned to teaching but I wasn’t sure how I was going to proceed with the topic.
