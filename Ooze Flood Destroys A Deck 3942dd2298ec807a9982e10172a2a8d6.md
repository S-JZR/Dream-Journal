I was working with three people to construct a wooden deck on a grassy hill for a slightly elevated remote house owned by a woman using power tools at night. The sky was overcast with dark grey clouds. After finishing the deck it began to rain heavily and the owner approached me.

> **Woman**: You’re letting everything get wet!
> 

> **Woman**: Why are you still working on the deck when you can build the awning?
> 

A flash flood of black ooze flowed down the hill and beneath the house. It tore off all the deck planks except for two and swept them away. The other workers decided to tear off the remaining planks in defeat since there was no point in continuing. As we watched the planks descend further down the hill we noticed that the ooze was heading towards a primary school that was still running.

> **Man**: The ooze is heading towards that school!
> 

> **Man**: We need to warn everyone!
> 

We sprinted past one block of pitch black buildings until notching an illuminated classroom at a lower elevation with an open door. Students were sitting at desks inside listening to a female teacher at the front of the room. We entered the classroom.

> **Group**: Flood! Flood! Flood! Run there is a flood!
> 

Everyone ignored us and acted like we weren't there. I was worried they were struggling to understand because we were shouting over each other but they simply didn't believe us.

The ooze emerged from the corner of the higher buildings and travelled towards us. Everyone panicked when they started to hear and see the ooze and fled.
