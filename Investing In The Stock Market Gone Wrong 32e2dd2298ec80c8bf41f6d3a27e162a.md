I was one of many secondary students seated on a bus when I opened a stock trading app on my phone to to experiment with investing in the stock market. I made sure you use an app with downside protection to avoid going into negatives. I didn’t know what I wanted to invest in and accidentally came across a “civil defence” stock owned by the government that covered city maintenance, managing riots, and funding wars. A label on the screen also said that it was part of the S&P 500 which gave me more confidence that it would perform well.

I invested $2,000 in the stocks and was prepared to lose it all. After an hour I checked the app and was surprised to see the stocks were up $1,000 and looked for a way to sell them because I was worried it was a hype that wouldn’t last but the buttons were absurdly small. After 3 minutes I found the sell request button but the stocks instantly went down $1000 and never moved beyond fluctuating up and down $20. I considered that stock market growth is a long-term process and if I’m going to impulsively fear every short-term change then I’m misguided.
