# Description

I was walking into an apartment room, representing an abandoned Reddit community owned by a fictional famous male tech influencer who went missing from the public eye, and took ownership of it. The room featured:

- cream walls
- grey carpet
- dining room:
    - long table with chairs
- middle room:
    - sliding glass doors overlooking a grass field and a public pool with bleachers and awnings
    - computer on a wooden desk to the right of the doors
- lounge room:
    - brown sofa
    - TV
    - split system air conditioner
    - door to a side hallway.

I noticed various adults and children, representing posts that were still pending approval for decades, standing outside on a ledge. A square and rectangular slab of concrete was floating in the air beside the ledge and half of the square blended into the grassy terrain below like an optical illusion. A child began to hop onto the slabs without falling and everyone was concerned for their safety.

> **Me**: Get back here, I don’t want you fall and die!
> 

> **Me**: That is cartoon physics and I’ll just fall straight through the floor so I won't follow you.
> 

I noticed the tech influencer on top of the bleachers filming a comeback video titled “How is it going?”

> **Man 1**: [Discussing miscellaneous things].
> 

> **Man 1**: I once made a videogame in the 1990’s with an easter egg for Gabe Newell but he never played it.
> 

He slid down a 50 metre wide slide into the pool on an inflatable ring. The video went viral and on the computer screen Gabe Newell was live streaming himself playing the videogame. The tech influencer remotely triggered the easter egg of Gabe as a Photo-Puppet performing a light-hearted mockery with censored swearing which he enjoyed.

In the lounge room I talked to five children seated on the floor about how technology has evolved.

> **Me**: When I grew up we used to have thick TVs but now they’re paper thin.
> 

> **Me**: We had VCRs that you slid VHS tapes into and rewound after watching.
> 

> **Me**: We had cars with tape players.
> 

A male and female scientist wearing white lab coats randomly entered from the hallway.

> **Man**: There is rare documentation of defective air conditioners emitting a silent hum that drove people to suicide.
> 

A girl burst into tears and went to the male scientist who called her.

> **Man 2**: Come here, what’s wrong?
> 

It reminded me of watching a hygiene film in primary school that showed dust mites everywhere like on teddy bears and I started distancing myself from them.
