# Description

I was with many secondary and primary school students in a school located within a city when our male teacher spoke to us.

> **Man 1**: Today we’re going on an excursion to the local discount store.
> 

Everyone walked through multiple city blocks and a large park until we encountered a footpath in front of a long 8-lane divided road leading to the store and a signalled pedestrian crossing to the far left. The teacher remained at the back of the line throughout the journey and couldn’t supervise anyone at the front. Some students exploited the opportunity to cross the road early to reach the other side. There was barely any traffic as only one car passed at a time in both directions. A young girl sprinted across without looking both ways and was nearly hit by a car passing behind her back. Three older students looked both ways once and sprinted across the road as other students blindly followed them, assuming they knew what they were doing and were nearly hit by cars passing in front and behind them. When the teacher arrived he was oblivious to what happened and those who remained crossed together.

> **Man 1**: The traffic has stopped so let’s cross now.
> 

While inside the store I was confused by why everyone was holding books so I asked a nearby male student.

> **Me**: Why are you carrying a book?
> 

> **Man 2**: We’re returning bestselling new releases that we don’t want.
> 

> **Man 1**: Can everyone please line up at the return desk to hand in your books.
> 

I felt embarrassed for being oblivious and while everyone was waiting in line I decided to make the trip worthwhile by browsing for items I could take with me like candy. However, when returning to the checkouts I realized that everyone left without me.
