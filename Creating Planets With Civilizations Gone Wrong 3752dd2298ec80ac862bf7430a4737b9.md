# Description

I was the captain of a spaceship seated in the cockpit beside the co-pilot where I pressed a button that emitted a giant white scanner ray into space. The ray created 9 different coloured planets full of people and futuristic buildings in layers using a random seed.

I assumed the role of a person living on the planet standing beside a river when I was aware that a blizzard was approaching the planet. I power walked past city streets and café towards a skyscraper and repeatedly warned everyone and made sure to change my messages to ensure they better received by clearly explained the call to action and reason why. However, nobody reacted.

> **Me**: Run!
> 

> **Me**: Get inside!
> 

> **Me**: A blizzard is about to hit the planet!
> 

I hid inside the skyscraper as the blizzard impacted the planet and instantly froze everything outside and covered it in a thin layer of snow. I found 2 tiny thermal purple gel balls in the first aid room inside that were designed to emit a field of heat for one person in such an emergency. I rubbed them once to activate them and held them in my hands which allowed me to comfortably move around outside where I witnessed the carnage first-hand.

3 of the other planets turned aggressive and exploited the vulnerability of other planets by waging wars to conquer everything.

I assumed the role of the captain again and discussed with my advisors how we should resolve the problem.

> **Man 1**: It’s probably too impractical to resolve the war since we don’t know how long it will last and can’t individually check who is good or bad.
> 

> **Me**: We could restart from scratch and remove the planets we know are inclined to violence.
> 

> **Man 2**: At least 2 planets must exist that will collaborate and challenge each others’ ideas.
> 

> **Me**: We should reuse the same seed to ensure the same people will form again.
> 

I hovered my finger over the button again when I had many personal convictions including:

- Is it wrong to kill people if we can perfectly recreate them?
- Do memories and lived experiences have any value?
- Is it wrong to kill an immoral majority along with a few innocent people?
- Will this resolve long-term problems or will they arise again in unforeseen ways?
- Is it immoral to create people who will experience suffering from nature and war?

I looked over at my co-pilot.

> **Me**: Are we the monsters?
> 

I continued to press the button and the planets were overwritten with 6 new planets and we flew away into space.
